Get help across coordination, training, knowledge sharing, monitoring, and digital hub activities for existing, emerging, and future Soil Living Labs and Lighthouses across Europe.

## How to use this chat

Ask a question and you will receive contextual answers with references to the indexed source documents. In the **same chat thread**, you can ask me follow-up questions (for example, “can you explain that further?”). I can remember the last few exchanges in your conversation to help interpret what you mean.

My answers are generated with [Mistral AI](https://mistral.ai/), in line with common practice for European-funded projects.

## How can I help you?

My knowledge focuses on living labs and lighthouses for soil health, focussing on frameworks, methodologies, tools, and monitoring approaches. I can receive questions and respond in a number of European languages - s'il vous plaît, essayez !, although English is my main one. Here’s what I can tell you about:

### Living Labs and Lighthouses Definitions and Frameworks

Living labs are open innovation arrangements where actors with different perspectives co-create solutions in real-life conditions, organised around co-creation with users in real-life contexts. They operate across three levels: macro (policy and societal context), meso (project-level activities), and micro (day-to-day operations and user engagement). Soil Health Lighthouses are experimental sites that aim to demonstrate and scale sustainable soil health practices, acting as hubs for innovation, knowledge exchange, and collaboration at local, regional, national, and international levels.

### Monitoring and Evaluation Frameworks

I can provide detailed guidance on assessing the progress and impact of living labs and lighthouses, for example around:
**Governance and Strategic Processes:** How frequently strategic decisions are made, partner contributions, and responsibilities.
**Human Resources:** Roles such as soil scientists, agronomists, innovation advisors, training coordinators, and outreach specialists.
**Equipment and Infrastructure:** The types of resources required, such as soil testing equipment, laboratories, agricultural tools, data management tools, and communication infrastructure.
**Business Models**: Elements like problems and solutions offered, stakeholder segments, costs and revenues (including grants, private funding, public funding, and revenues from services), and metrics for monitoring impacts.
**Data Management and IP:** How data is shared, licensed, and protected, including collaborative agreements, confidentiality, open-source licensing, and adherence to principles like OCAP®.
**User Engagement and Co-Creation:** Methods for involving users in real-life contexts, including iterative living lab processes and the inclusion of post-human stakeholders (e.g., ecosystems, future generations).
**Learning Materials and Capacity Building:** A wide range of materials such as academic papers, apps, best practice descriptions, datasets, e-courses, field visit materials, infographics, mentoring programs, policy briefs, and more.
**Monitoring Frequency:** How often parameters like soil health, stakeholder satisfaction, and equipment availability are and should be monitored.

### Knowledge Sharing and Capacity Building
I also know about indicators for measuring the success of knowledge sharing and capacity-building activities. Thes can include:

- The number of educational resources (e.g., courses, toolkits, training sessions).
- User satisfaction with knowledge sharing and capacity-building efforts.
- The existence of repositories or "knowledge hubs" for sharing best practices.
